# Report-First Authoring Repeatability Check

## 1. Purpose

This document checks whether the report-first Leadership Approach model appears repeatable beyond the first full Process-led benchmark report.

The review compares one full canonical benchmark report and three draft canonical report briefs. The purpose is product/editorial QA only. It does not approve production implementation, runtime changes, renderer work, import logic, schema changes, PDF generation, package promotion, or workbook compilation.

## 5. Core Thesis Comparison

`process_results_people_vision` says the leader creates confidence by giving work a clearer way forward and making sure it produces practical movement.

`results_process_people_vision` says the leader creates confidence by moving work out of discussion and into visible traction, with Process preventing urgency from becoming brittle.

`people_process_results_vision` says the leader creates confidence by creating the human conditions for ownership, with Process turning trust into coordination.

`vision_people_process_results` says the leader creates confidence by giving work a larger frame, with People making the direction shared and human.

The theses are meaningfully differentiated. Each pattern starts from a different leadership instinct: structure, movement, ownership, or horizon.

## 7. Development Edge Comparison

| Pattern | Development edge |
|---|---|
| `process_results_people_vision` | Make structure more shared and meaningful before asking people to own it. |
| `results_process_people_vision` | Make momentum owned, evidence-aware, and directionally meaningful. |
| `people_process_results_vision` | Let trust produce timely decisions, candour, and visible traction. |
| `vision_people_process_results` | Make future direction concrete, sequenced, and measurable enough to act on. |

The development edges are distinct and credible. None asks the reader to become a different kind of leader; each broadens the existing pattern.

## 8. Risk Of Repetition Or Generic Language

Current repetition risk is manageable. The briefs share the same proof structure, but their core meaning, hero titles, pressure behaviours, and development moves are sufficiently different.

The main risk for the next authoring stage is structural sameness. If every full report follows identical paragraph rhythm and list cadence, the content could still feel templated even when the interpretation is distinct. The next authoring pass should vary chapter openings, examples, and closing cadence while preserving the shared report architecture.

Generic language risk is lowest in the Results-led and People-led briefs because the behavioural tensions are concrete. The Vision-led brief is the highest risk because future-oriented copy can easily become abstract or motivational. It currently avoids that by repeatedly translating Vision into route, ownership, and evidence, but it should receive especially close QA when expanded.

No brief frames lower-ranked signals as weakness. Each treats lower-ranked signals as range, extension, or practical broadening.

## 10. Recommendation On Repeatability

Report-first authoring appears repeatable enough to continue the proof.

The three briefs show that the model can produce distinct Leadership Approach interpretations across different first-ranked signals without falling back into signal glossary language or a simple name-swapping pattern. The model still needs a second-stage proof: expand at least one of these briefs into a full canonical report and compare all four for cadence, repetition, and PDF-readiness.

Recommended next step: expand the strongest new brief, `results_process_people_vision`, into a full canonical report, then perform a side-by-side QA against the Process-led benchmark before creating schema, renderer, import, PDF, package, or runtime work.

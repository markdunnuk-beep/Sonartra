# Full 24-report harmonisation notes

## Readability changes

The P12F harmonisation pass reviewed all 24 Leadership Approach report-first canonical reports for plain-English readability, visible template seams, internal editorial leakage, and the specific jargon terms flagged for review.

The pass did not rewrite the report set or change the report-first structure. It focused on sentences where the meaning was commercially sound but harder to picture than necessary. The strongest edits replaced abstract labels with concrete work language: proof, progress, working path, review rhythm, handovers, decision rights, customer consequence, and people carrying the work.

## Jargon reduced

Reduced or replaced repeated uses of:

- "development edge" and "growth edge" with "main growth move" or a direct explanation.
- "operating discipline" with "working discipline", "working method", or "working structure" where clearer.
- "operating route" with "working path", "way of working", or "path".
- "visible movement" with "visible progress".
- "delivery recovery" with "getting delivery back on track" in reader-facing examples.
- "testable" with "clear enough to prove", "checked", or "easy to judge" where the sentence benefited.
- "human conditions" with clearer people/work wording.
- "socially fragile" with "easy to lose trust in".
- "broadening alignment" with "getting more people genuinely on board".

## Signal-family consistency

The underlying signal meanings remain unchanged:

- Results still means progress, proof, decisions, outcomes, and evidence that work is changing.
- Process still means clear ways of working, roles, standards, handovers, review rhythm, and repeatability.
- Vision still means future direction, capability, customer promise, why the work matters, and strategic judgement.
- People still means trust, ownership, contribution, local judgement, commitment, and how people carry the work.

The pass kept some repeated family language where it supports standalone comprehension. A reader only receives one report, so clarity took priority over forced variation.

## Remaining editorial watchlist

The phrase "future capability" remains in several reports because it is often the clearest commercial shorthand for what Vision contributes. It should still be watched in future editorial passes and replaced with "capability being built" or a specific customer/operating capability when a sentence feels abstract.

"Cadence" also remains in places where it refers to meeting or review rhythm. Future edits should prefer "review rhythm" or "meeting rhythm" when the surrounding sentence is already abstract.

The next full editorial pass should focus on reading several reports aloud on mobile, not on further global term replacement.

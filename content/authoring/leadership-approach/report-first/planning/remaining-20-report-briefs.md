## Results-led missing patterns

### results_process_vision_people

1. Pattern key: `results_process_vision_people`
2. Ranked signal order:
   - Rank 1: Results
   - Rank 2: Process
   - Rank 3: Vision
   - Rank 4: People
3. Core leadership move: Creates visible progress quickly, then builds enough structure for that progress to become repeatable and strategically useful.
4. Primary value: Helps teams leave unresolved discussion and move into accountable action that can be tested, reviewed, and connected to a larger direction.
5. Likely team experience: Others may experience this pattern as decisive and stabilising, especially when work has drifted, but may also feel that the human impact is considered after the route is already moving.
6. Decision style: Looks for the decision that creates traction, then checks whether the route has enough ownership, sequence, and review discipline to hold.
7. Communication style: Communicates through priorities, next steps, standards, and practical direction; the strongest version also explains what the progress is meant to build.
8. Pressure behaviour: Tightens toward pace, closure, control of priorities, and pressure to prove movement, with less time for emotional processing or shared interpretation.
9. Rank 3 expansion: Vision adds strategic framing so progress is not only visible but meaningful, helping action connect to capability, direction, or future advantage.
10. Rank 4 expansion: People adds the deliberate check that progress is understood and carried by the team, not only driven by a clear plan.
11. Main development edge: Bring People in before the action hardens, so commitment and concern can shape the route rather than react to it.
12. Distinction from similar patterns: Compared with `results_process_people_vision`, this pattern should make Vision the first expansion. The report should emphasise direction and strategic usefulness before team ownership, while still making People the main further growth edge.
13. Avoided generic trap: Do not write this as "Results plus Process equals delivery discipline" with Vision and People tacked on as soft additions.
14. Final line direction: Progress becomes most powerful when the team can see both where it is going and where they belong in carrying it.

## Cross-pattern differentiation notes

### Results-led reports versus Process-led reports

Results-led reports should begin with movement, closure, evidence, and the gap between intention and execution. Process may stabilise that movement, but the first instinct is to make progress real.

Process-led reports should begin with structure, sequence, ownership, standards, and the way work holds together. Results may sharpen the structure, but the first instinct is to make the route dependable.

Do not let both families collapse into generic delivery language. Results-led pressure should feel like urgency, output, closure, and proof. Process-led pressure should feel like control of the method, sequencing, governance, and tightening the route.

### Vision-led reports versus People-led reports

Vision-led reports should begin with future direction, meaning, strategic possibility, and what the work is meant to build. People may help the future become shared, but the first instinct is to orient the work toward a horizon.

People-led reports should begin with trust, ownership, contribution, motivation, and the human conditions that make work possible. Vision may give that commitment direction, but the first instinct is to bring people with the work.

Do not make Vision-led reports simply inspirational or People-led reports simply supportive. Vision needs practical translation. People needs real leadership accountability.

### How rank 2 changes the whole pattern

Rank 2 is not a supporting adjective. It changes the lead signal's operating logic:
- Results plus Process creates structured traction.
- Results plus Vision creates purposeful momentum.
- Results plus People creates mobilised progress.
- Process plus Results creates delivery discipline.
- Process plus Vision creates strategic operating design.
- Process plus People creates trusted ways of working.
- Vision plus Results creates proof of the future.
- Vision plus Process creates architecture for the future.
- Vision plus People creates shared belief.
- People plus Results creates committed action.
- People plus Process creates trusted coordination.
- People plus Vision creates belonging around a direction.

### How rank 3 should feel like expansion

Rank 3 should be written as a real leadership broadening move, not a light add-on. It is the first deliberate range signal. In full reports, Chapter 8 should show what becomes possible when the leader brings rank 3 forward earlier than instinct would normally allow.

Rank 3 should change the quality of the pattern:
- Vision at rank 3 adds meaning, horizon, and future usefulness.
- People at rank 3 adds ownership, trust, and participative judgement.
- Process at rank 3 adds rhythm, accountability, and repeatability.
- Results at rank 3 adds prioritisation, proof, and closure.

### How rank 4 should become the main deliberate growth edge

Rank 4 should not be treated as a weakness label. It is the furthest extension and the highest-leverage development edge. In full reports, it should answer: what does this leader need to bring in deliberately when the lead pair is already working?

Rank 4 should often drive the most specific development advice:
- People last: involve people before the route, action, or direction hardens.
- Vision last: name the future purpose before work becomes only immediate or operational.
- Process last: build the method before energy, belief, or action becomes fragmented.
- Results last: force visible progress before alignment, structure, or purpose becomes too comfortable.

### Risks of making the 24 reports feel templated

The biggest risk is permutation writing: swapping signal names into the same sentences. Avoid repeated formulas such as "X leads, Y supports, Z adds range." Each report needs its own commercial situation, pressure pattern, decision behaviour, and development edge.

Other risks:
- Overusing "clarity", "ownership", "meaning", and "momentum" without changing their specific function.
- Making every lower-ranked signal a generic coaching suggestion.
- Treating People as warmth, Vision as inspiration, Process as admin, and Results as speed.
- Giving every pattern the same pressure behaviour with different labels.
- Writing chapter structures that sound identical across the 24 reports.

# Premium Result Language Quality Rubric

Use this rubric to score Pattern Dossiers, Field Mapping Matrices, staged PSV rows, preview reports, and report-first canonical editorial reports.

Target:
- Minimum: `8.5/10`
- Preferred for report-first canonical outputs: `9+/10`

## Scoring Scale

### 1-3: Not Usable

Vague, inaccurate, generic, structurally incoherent, or detached from the ranked-pattern model. It may define signals instead of interpreting the reader's pattern, use unsupported scoring logic, or sound like unreviewed AI output.

### 4-6: Structurally Plausible But Weak

Broadly follows the section purpose but lacks behavioural specificity, leadership context, pressure insight, or pattern-level coherence. Safe but forgettable.

### 6.5/10: Competent But Generic Or Modular

Understandable and mostly correct, but feels assembled from interchangeable parts. It may mention the right signals and ranks while failing to create strong reader recognition, practical leadership insight, or a coherent end-to-end interpretation.

### 7-8.4: Strong But Not Yet Paid-Quality

Useful and mostly specific, with recognisable leadership behaviour and reasonable section flow. Still needs sharper team impact, decision behaviour, communication behaviour, pressure behaviour, or stronger interpretation.

### 8.5/10: Paid-Quality

Behaviourally specific, coherent, reader-useful, and commercially credible. Explains the reader's leadership pattern in a way that feels recognisable, practical, and differentiated from generic leadership content. Lower-ranked signals are framed as range.

### 9+/10: Premium And Differentiated

Memorable, precise, commercially credible, and clearly authored. Creates a differentiated reader experience with behavioural insight, practical development value, strong coherence, and re-read value. Suitable for a paid premium report without apology.

## Scoring Categories

| category | high-score definition | low-score warning |
|---|---|---|
| Behavioural specificity | Names observable leadership behaviour: what the reader notices, asks, protects, delays, closes, communicates, or overuses. | Defines signals abstractly or uses broad trait language. |
| Leadership applicability | Applies to real leadership work. | Could describe almost any professional. |
| Reader recognition | Helps the reader recognise themselves without flattery or fixed labels. | Plausible but not personally recognisable. |
| Non-generic insight | Provides pattern-specific meaning tied to rank order and score shape where relevant. | Interchangeable across patterns. |
| Pressure behaviour | Explains what changes when stakes rise or time compresses. | Omits pressure or treats it vaguely. |
| Team impact | Shows effects on ownership, trust, clarity, delivery, morale, or alignment. | Describes only the individual leader. |
| Decision behaviour | Explains decisions, closure, trade-offs, and judgement. | Gives generic decision advice. |
| Communication behaviour | Shows framing, listening, expectation setting, context, or buy-in. | Treats communication as a generic soft skill. |
| Practical development value | Gives usable actions that broaden the pattern without shaming the reader. | Tells the reader to become a different type of leader. |
| Coherence | Sections build progressively without contradiction or repetitive filler. | Sections repeat or drift. |
| Premium tone | Calm, adult, specific, commercially credible, and polished. | Motivational, therapeutic, inflated, or patronising. |
| Absence of modular feel | Reads as deliberately authored from a full interpretation. | Feels stitched together from templated clauses. |
| Ranked-pattern preservation | Preserves rank order and lower-rank range framing. | Reorders signals or treats lower ranks as deficits. |
| Score-shape sensitivity | Uses score shape only where it improves reader value. | Forces variation that weakens quality. |
| Report-first coherence | Reads as one premium editorial report. | Feels like UI assembly of blocks. |
| PDF suitability | Has enough structure, pacing, and completeness to work as a reference-style PDF. | Only works as short web cards. |

## Pass Criteria

A result should not be promoted unless:

- overall score is at least `8.5/10`
- report-first canonical outputs target `9+/10`
- no central category scores below `8/10`
- no prohibited model drift is present
- lower-ranked signals are framed as useful range
- copy reads as leadership pattern interpretation, not signal explanation
- the result feels authored, not mechanically assembled

## Failure Conditions

Revise before approval if:

- lower-ranked signals are framed as weakness, deficiency, absence, or failure
- the copy relies on generic leadership language
- the result could fit most other ranked patterns with minimal change
- the report explains the model more than the reader
- score-shape logic implies a change to scoring or rank order
- writing sounds like modular AI output
- the closing does not feel earned
- development guidance is vague or detached from the pattern
- the report does not feel commercially credible as a paid result

## Manual QA Decision Labels

### PASS

The output meets the required standard and can move to the next workflow stage.

### PASS WITH MINOR EDITORIAL NOTES

The output is strong enough to proceed, but specific wording or structure should be refined before final promotion.

### REQUIRES REVISION

The output should not proceed until the identified quality, structure, or drift issues are corrected.

### FAIL

The output does not meet the required editorial or product standard and should be re-authored from the source interpretation.

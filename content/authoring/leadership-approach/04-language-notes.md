# Leadership Approach Language Notes

Use these notes for all Leadership Approach authoring tasks.

## Plain Behavioural Intelligence

Write in British English. Use clear, adult, practical language. The reader should understand the point on first read.

Prioritise:

1. immediate clarity
2. recognition
3. behavioural specificity
4. practical usefulness
5. calm authority
6. editorial polish last

Use observable behaviour:

- what the leader notices
- what they protect
- what they start with
- what they delay
- what they overuse
- what they need to invite earlier
- what others may experience
- what practical consequence follows

## Avoid

Avoid:

- abstract premium fog
- motivational slogans
- hero language
- therapy language
- consultancy language
- moral judgement
- leadership celebrity language
- fixed identity labels
- capability scoring language
- rank 4 deficit framing
- making balanced profiles vague
- making paired profiles rank-1 dominant

Avoid phrases such as:

- leadership DNA
- executive presence
- high-performance mindset
- unlock potential
- command the room
- servant leader
- people person
- visionary genius
- process person
- born leader
- natural authority
- low people focus
- weak on process
- lacks vision
- not results-driven

## Preferred Phrasing

Use phrases such as:

- "You may lead first by..."
- "This can help when..."
- "Watch for the point where..."
- "A useful next step is..."
- "Bring in..."
- "This is range to add deliberately..."
- "The work may narrow when..."
- "Others may experience this as..."
- "The practical value is..."
- "When pressure rises, you may..."

Do not overuse any one phrase. Vary sentence openings.

## Signal Guardrails

### Results

Results should mean outcomes, decision, delivery, progress, and follow-through.

Do:

- connect Results to clarity, accountability, and movement
- show the practical value of making decisions visible
- describe pressure risks as rushing, narrowing, or skipping context

Do not:

- make Results sound aggressive
- frame it as dominance
- suggest the leader does not care about people
- treat delivery as the only serious form of leadership

### Process

Process should mean structure, sequencing, standards, repeatability, and coordination.

Do:

- connect Process to trust, quality, and reduced rework
- show how structure helps people act with more confidence
- describe overuse as over-structuring or delaying movement

Do not:

- make Process sound bureaucratic
- reduce it to paperwork
- frame it as rigidity or control
- imply it lacks judgement

### Vision

Vision should mean future direction, framing, possibility, and meaning.

Do:

- connect Vision to direction, coherence, and longer-term choices
- show how it helps people understand why work matters
- describe overuse as opening too many possibilities or staying too high-level

Do not:

- make Vision sound vague, magical, or purely inspirational
- reduce it to creativity or branding
- frame it as superior to practical leadership

### People

People should mean trust, communication, inclusion, development, and shared commitment.

Do:

- connect People to trust, buy-in, clarity, and sustainable performance
- show how people awareness changes leadership decisions
- describe overuse as over-accommodating or delaying hard calls

Do not:

- make People sound like popularity
- frame it as extroversion
- imply softness or lack of standards
- reduce it to being liked

## Rank And Score Shape Guardrails

Rank 1 is the clearest starting point. Do not make it sound like the only valid route.

Rank 2 is the main shaping influence. In paired profiles, treat rank 1 and rank 2 as a close pair.

Rank 3 is useful support or balancing range. It may help the leader adjust, recover, or broaden the approach.

Rank 4 is deliberate range. It is not a weakness, deficit, flaw, or absence. Write it as a condition to bring in when the main route has become too narrow.

Concentrated profiles should have a clear main route without sounding rigid.

Paired profiles should read as a genuine two-signal partnership.

Graduated profiles should respect the full order without sounding like a status ladder.

Balanced profiles should be specific. Explain how the leader can move between Results, Process, Vision, and People depending on the situation.

## Section Distinction

Each section should add a new layer:

- Orientation: quick pattern recognition
- Recognition: how the reader may recognise it in real leadership work
- Signal Roles: what each signal contributes at each rank
- Pattern Mechanics: how the pattern works in motion
- Pattern Synthesis: central meaning, gift, trap, and takeaway
- Strengths: what becomes easier when the pattern works well
- Narrowing: where it can overuse or miss range
- Application: practical ways to use it
- Closing Integration: final synthesis and take-forward line

Do not copy language from one section into another unless the repetition is structurally required.

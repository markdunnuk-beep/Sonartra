# Premium Result Language Quality Rubric

Use this rubric to score Pattern Dossiers, Field Mapping Matrices, staged PSV rows, and rendered preview reports. The target for paid Leadership Approach output is at least `8.5/10`.

## Scoring Scale

### 1-3: Not Usable

Language is vague, inaccurate, generic, structurally incoherent, or detached from the ranked-pattern model. It may define signals instead of interpreting the reader's pattern, use unsupported scoring logic, or sound like unreviewed AI output.

### 4-6: Structurally Plausible But Weak

Language broadly follows the section purpose but lacks behavioural specificity, leadership context, pressure insight, or pattern-level coherence. It may be safe but forgettable.

### 6.5/10: Competent But Generic Or Modular

The copy is understandable and mostly correct, but it feels assembled from interchangeable parts. It may mention the right signals and ranks while failing to create strong reader recognition, practical leadership insight, or a coherent end-to-end interpretation.

### 7-8.4: Strong But Not Yet Paid-Quality

The copy is useful and mostly specific, with recognisable leadership behaviour and reasonable section flow. It may still need sharper team impact, decision behaviour, communication behaviour, pressure behaviour, or stronger score-shape interpretation.

### 8.5/10: Paid-Quality

The copy is behaviourally specific, coherent, reader-useful, and commercially credible. It explains the reader's leadership pattern in a way that feels recognisable, practical, and differentiated from generic leadership content. Lower-ranked signals are framed as range, and score shape affects interpretation without changing scoring.

### 9+/10: Premium And Differentiated

The copy is memorable, precise, and commercially credible. It creates a differentiated reader experience with clear behavioural insight, practical development value, and strong coherence across sections. It feels authored, not assembled. It could plausibly sit inside a paid premium report without apology.

## Scoring Categories

Score each category from `1` to `10`, then use editorial judgement for the final score.

| category | high-score definition | low-score warning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Behavioural specificity | Names observable leadership behaviour: what the reader notices, asks, protects, delays, closes, communicates, or overuses. | Defines signals abstractly or uses broad trait language. |
| Leadership applicability | Clearly applies to real leadership work, not general personality description. | Could describe almost any professional or team member. |
| Reader recognition | Helps the reader recognise themselves without flattery or fixed labels. | Sounds plausible but not personally recognisable. |
| Non-generic insight | Provides pattern-specific meaning tied to rank order and score shape. | Feels interchangeable across patterns. |
| Pressure behaviour | Explains what changes when stakes rise, time compresses, or uncertainty increases. | Omits pressure or only says "under pressure this may be harder". |
| Team impact | Shows how the pattern affects ownership, trust, clarity, delivery, morale, or alignment. | Describes only the individual leader. |
| Decision behaviour | Explains how the pattern shapes decisions, closure, trade-offs, or judgement. | Gives generic decision advice. |
| Communication behaviour | Shows how the pattern affects framing, listening, expectation setting, context, or buy-in. | Treats communication as a generic soft skill. |
| Practical development value | Gives usable actions that broaden the pattern without shaming the reader. | Offers vague advice or tells the reader to become a different type of leader. |
| Coherence across sections | Sections build progressively without contradiction or repetitive filler. | Sections repeat each other or drift into different interpretations. |
| Premium tone | Calm, adult, specific, commercially credible, and polished. | Motivational, therapeutic, jargon-heavy, inflated, or patronising. |
| Absence of modular/AI-generated feel | Reads as deliberately authored from a full interpretation. | Feels stitched together from templated clauses. |
| Preservation of ranked-pattern meaning | Preserves rank order, four-signal model, pattern key, and lower-rank range framing. | Reorders signals, implies scoring changes, or treats lower ranks as deficits. |
| Score-shape sensitivity | Uses concentrated, paired, graduated, or balanced shape to adjust interpretation. | Ignores score shape or treats it as a separate runtime classification. |

## Pass Criteria

A field map or staged PSV row should not be promoted unless it meets all of these conditions:

- final score is at least `8.5/10` for premium sections
- no category central to the section purpose scores below `8/10`
- no prohibited model drift is present
- score-shape language affects interpretation only
- lower-ranked signals are framed as useful range
- the copy reads as leadership pattern interpretation, not signal explanation

# Leadership Approach Pattern Dossier Template

Use this template for future premium Leadership Approach Pattern Dossiers. A dossier is the canonical authoring source for one ranked pattern and score-shape focus before any Field Mapping Matrix or PSV row is created.

Do not use this template to introduce runtime scoring logic, thresholds as authoring logic, archetypes, overlay dimensions, alternate result paths, or UI-side interpretation. The dossier explains the reader's ranked leadership pattern only.

## 1. Pattern identity

**Source anchor:** `pattern_identity`

- **Intended purpose:** Name the ranked leadership pattern and give the reader immediate orientation.
- **Required content:** Rank 1 through rank 4 signal roles, the score-shape focus, and the central leadership question underneath the pattern.
- **Quality bar:** The reader should recognise the pattern as a leadership route, not a personality label or signal list.
- **Common failure modes:** Raw `pattern_key` language, archetype naming, generic trait description, rank 4 deficit framing, or score-shape wording that implies a scoring change.

## 2. Core leadership thesis

**Source anchor:** `core_leadership_thesis`

- **Intended purpose:** State the main interpretation that holds the whole dossier together.
- **Required content:** The pattern's core leadership contribution, the interaction between the top signals, and the main developmental edge.
- **Quality bar:** Reads as a distinctive thesis for this exact rank order and score shape.
- **Common failure modes:** Broad leadership advice, signal definitions, old pair-oriented interpretation, or a thesis that could fit any pattern.

## 3. What your leadership naturally pays attention to

**Source anchor:** `attention_pattern`

- **Intended purpose:** Explain what the reader is likely to notice first in real leadership work.
- **Required content:** Observable attention habits, meeting or work cues, practical questions the leader may ask, and what lower-ranked signals may be noticed later.
- **Quality bar:** Behaviourally specific enough to support Orientation, Recognition, and Mechanics fields.
- **Common failure modes:** Abstract cognition language, generic "focuses on..." phrasing, or lower-ranked signals written as absent.

## 4. How your leadership creates value

**Source anchor:** `value_creation`

- **Intended purpose:** Explain the practical value the pattern creates for teams and organisations.
- **Required content:** Work contexts where the pattern helps, team or delivery consequences, and the value of the top-ranked interaction.
- **Quality bar:** Commercially credible and grounded in leadership behaviour rather than praise.
- **Common failure modes:** Flattery, motivational copy, generic strengths, or value claims unsupported by the ranked pattern.

## 5. How your style feels to other people

**Source anchor:** `felt_experience`

- **Intended purpose:** Describe how colleagues and teams may experience the leadership pattern.
- **Required content:** Positive felt impact, possible tension, and how the pattern affects ownership, trust, contribution, or confidence.
- **Quality bar:** Balanced, recognisable, and specific without moral judgement.
- **Common failure modes:** Overclaiming, therapy language, personality criticism, or one-sided praise.

## 6. Decision behaviour

**Source anchor:** `decision_behaviour`

- **Intended purpose:** Explain how the pattern shapes decisions, closure, trade-offs, and judgement.
- **Required content:** How the leader tends to decide, what makes a decision feel sound, the decision strength, and the decision risk.
- **Quality bar:** Specific enough to generate reader-useful decision guidance later.
- **Common failure modes:** Generic decision advice, capability scoring, aggressive Results framing, or unsupported claims about decisiveness.

## 7. Communication behaviour

**Source anchor:** `communication_behaviour`

- **Intended purpose:** Explain how the pattern shapes communication and shared understanding.
- **Required content:** Natural communication style, what it clarifies, what may be under-communicated, and how to broaden the communication.
- **Quality bar:** Shows practical communication behaviour, not generic "communicates well" content.
- **Common failure modes:** Soft-skill generalities, extroversion assumptions, slogans, or advice detached from rank order.

## 8. Pressure behaviour

**Source anchor:** `pressure_behaviour`

- **Intended purpose:** Explain what the pattern may do when stakes rise or time compresses.
- **Required content:** Pressure narrowing, short-term value, possible team cost, and useful range questions.
- **Quality bar:** Names pressure behaviour without shaming the reader or weakening the pattern's legitimate value.
- **Common failure modes:** Crisis stereotypes, moral judgement, vague stress language, or treating lower-ranked signals as deficiencies.

## 9. What your pattern protects

**Source anchor:** `protective_function`

- **Intended purpose:** Explain the leadership risk the pattern is naturally trying to protect against.
- **Required content:** Practical work risks, deeper protection function, value of the protection, and cost when overused.
- **Quality bar:** Protection should feel useful and mature, not defensive or pathological.
- **Common failure modes:** Fear-based diagnosis, generic risk lists, or protection framed as control for its own sake.

## 10. The hidden cost

**Source anchor:** `hidden_cost`

- **Intended purpose:** Explain the subtle cost of overusing the main pattern.
- **Required content:** What may be under-owned, under-explained, delayed, or narrowed when the top-ranked route dominates.
- **Quality bar:** Clear enough to feed Synthesis, Narrowing, and Closing Integration without sounding like a flaw.
- **Common failure modes:** Weakness framing, rank 4 deficit language, unsupported criticism, or generic "watch out" content.

## 11. Where the pattern narrows

**Source anchor:** `narrowing_pattern`

- **Intended purpose:** Show the specific overuse pattern and the contexts where more range is needed.
- **Required content:** Narrowed-state behaviours, how the narrowing shows up, and the growth move back into range.
- **Quality bar:** Specific, behavioural, and non-punitive.
- **Common failure modes:** Treating rank order as a status ladder, diagnosing motives, or making the reader sound broken.

## 12. How rank 3 expands the pattern

**Source anchor:** `rank_3_extension`

- **Intended purpose:** Explain how the third-ranked signal adds useful range to the main route.
- **Required content:** What rank 3 adds, when it matters, and how it changes the pattern without replacing it.
- **Quality bar:** Rank 3 should feel like practical extension, not a secondary weakness to fix.
- **Common failure modes:** Deficit language, generic balancing phrases, or writing rank 3 as equal to rank 1.

## 13. How rank 4 expands the pattern

**Source anchor:** `rank_4_extension`

- **Intended purpose:** Explain how the fourth-ranked signal adds deliberate range when the pattern narrows.
- **Required content:** What rank 4 contributes, when to bring it in, and how it strengthens the leadership pattern.
- **Quality bar:** Rank 4 is range to add deliberately, never absence, weakness, or failure.
- **Common failure modes:** "Low", "lacks", "weak", "missing", or any phrasing that turns rank 4 into a deficit.

## 14. Practical development moves

**Source anchor:** `development_moves`

- **Intended purpose:** Give practical ways to use and broaden the pattern in real leadership work.
- **Required content:** Same-day leadership actions, useful questions, decision/communication moves, and pressure checks.
- **Quality bar:** Advice should be immediately usable and clearly derived from the pattern.
- **Common failure modes:** Generic coaching, motivational slogans, therapy tone, or asking the reader to become a different type of leader.

## 15. Integrated closing interpretation

**Source anchor:** `integrated_closing`

- **Intended purpose:** Bring the full pattern, value, risk, and growth direction together in a final synthesis.
- **Required content:** Core gift, score-shape meaning, development edge, and how lower-ranked signals broaden the pattern.
- **Quality bar:** Feels like the earned conclusion of the dossier, not a summary stitched from earlier sections.
- **Common failure modes:** Repetition, new unsupported claims, generic conclusion, or runtime/source metadata leakage.

## 16. Memorable line

**Source anchor:** `memorable_line`

- **Intended purpose:** Leave one precise, memorable final line that captures the pattern.
- **Required content:** The pattern's core gift and growth edge in one grounded sentence.
- **Quality bar:** Memorable without becoming a slogan.
- **Common failure modes:** Motivational quote, brand tagline, generic encouragement, or language not specific to the rank order.

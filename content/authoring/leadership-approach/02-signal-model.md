# Leadership Approach Signal Model

## Signal Keys And Labels

- `results` - Results
- `process` - Process
- `vision` - Vision
- `people` - People

Pattern keys join the four signal keys in rank order, for example:

```text
results_process_vision_people
```

Use:

```powershell
cmd /c npx tsx scripts/authoring/list-reader-first-patterns.ts --signals results,process,vision,people
```

## Results

Results means leading through outcomes, direction, decision, progress, and follow-through.

It looks like:

- asking what needs to be achieved
- wanting decisions to turn into action
- noticing drift, delay, or unclear ownership
- keeping attention on delivery and consequences
- making progress visible
- protecting standards around completion

Results helps leadership by keeping work connected to outcome. It can make priorities clearer and reduce confusion about what matters now.

It can narrow when overused if the person pushes for delivery before people, process, or future direction are clear enough. It can narrow when neglected if the work stays busy without enough accountability or decision.

Others may notice:

- directness
- drive
- outcome focus
- impatience with drift
- a strong need for clarity on what will happen next

Do not frame Results as aggression, domination, or lack of care. Results is about outcome focus, not force.

## Process

Process means leading through structure, sequencing, standards, reliability, and the way work is organised.

It looks like:

- clarifying steps and responsibilities
- making work repeatable
- noticing gaps in method or handover
- protecting standards and consistency
- slowing down enough to reduce avoidable mistakes
- creating systems people can trust

Process helps leadership by making work easier to coordinate. It can turn intention into a reliable path.

It can narrow when overused if the person keeps refining structure after the situation needs movement, people contact, or a bigger frame. It can narrow when neglected if energy turns into confusion or rework.

Others may notice:

- steadiness
- order
- careful standards
- attention to risk
- preference for a clear way of working

Do not frame Process as bureaucracy, rigidity, or control. Process is about usable structure, not paperwork for its own sake.

## Vision

Vision means leading through future direction, framing, possibility, meaning, and seeing what the work could become.

It looks like:

- explaining why the work matters
- seeing longer-term implications
- reframing the current problem
- noticing possibility before others do
- connecting decisions to a wider direction
- making change feel more coherent

Vision helps leadership by giving people a reason to move. It can make choices feel connected rather than reactive.

It can narrow when overused if the person keeps opening future possibilities without enough decision, structure, or people grounding. It can narrow when neglected if the work becomes too short-term, mechanical, or flat.

Others may notice:

- future thinking
- framing
- imagination
- strategic context
- a tendency to lift the conversation above the immediate task

Do not frame Vision as fantasy, vagueness, or performance. Vision is about direction and meaning, not decoration.

## People

People means leading through trust, communication, inclusion, development, and how people are brought with the work.

It looks like:

- noticing morale, confidence, and buy-in
- making space for concerns or contribution
- helping people understand what is changing
- protecting trust during pressure
- adapting communication to the people involved
- developing others rather than only directing them

People helps leadership by making work sustainable through human commitment. It can improve trust, shared understanding, and practical cooperation.

It can narrow when overused if the person keeps accommodating concerns after the work needs a decision, structure, or clearer direction. It can narrow when neglected if delivery happens without enough trust or shared understanding.

Others may notice:

- empathy
- listening
- communication
- attention to team climate
- willingness to bring people into the work

Do not frame People as popularity, softness, extroversion, or avoiding standards. People is about trust and shared commitment, not being liked.

## Rank Role Guidance

### Rank 1

Rank 1 is the clearest leadership starting point. It should be written as the route the person may reach for first when leadership is needed.

Use language such as:

- "You may lead first by..."
- "Your clearest starting point is..."
- "This is likely to be the route you trust first..."

### Rank 2

Rank 2 is the main shaping influence. It should show what gives rank 1 more range, credibility, care, direction, or structure.

Use language such as:

- "This becomes more effective when..."
- "The second signal helps by..."
- "Together, these two signals may..."

### Rank 3

Rank 3 is useful support or balancing range. It can stabilise the pattern and help the leader adjust.

Use language such as:

- "This can help when..."
- "This gives the approach another way to..."
- "This may be useful when the main route starts to narrow..."

### Rank 4

Rank 4 is deliberate range. It is not a deficit or flaw.

Use language such as:

- "This may need to be added deliberately."
- "This is range to practise, not a weakness."
- "Bring this in when the main approach is missing..."

Do not make rank 4 sound absent, broken, or inferior.

## Score Shape Guidance

### Concentrated

One signal is clearly stronger. Treat rank 1 as the main leadership route. Keep the language clear, but do not make the leader sound one-dimensional.

### Paired

The top two signals are close. Treat them as a pair. Avoid writing as if rank 1 dominates and rank 2 merely supports.

### Graduated

The scores step down clearly. Treat the full rank order as meaningful. Avoid blunt hierarchy or status language.

### Balanced

All four scores are close. Read the rank order lightly. Do not make balanced sound vague. Name how the person can move between leadership routes based on what the situation needs.

## 4. Pattern Accuracy Audit

**Score: 9.0/10**

The report accurately preserves the rank order: Evidence first, Practicality second, Standards third, Judgement fourth. The opening, Key insight, Decision mechanics, Tightening, Range, and Development sections all repeat the correct sequence without turning it into a flat list of traits.

Evidence is represented well as the first trust point: what can be known, checked, observed, or relied on. It avoids making the reader cold or rigid.

Practicality is represented as translation into action and usable movement. It is not treated as shallow execution, though some repetition of `usable path` and `practical path` could make it feel more procedural than it needs to.

Standards is represented as a defensibility and quality-of-choice check. It avoids bureaucracy and moral superiority. The only risk is that Standards can occasionally feel like a late audit rather than a live third-rank force; RFA-14 should make Standards slightly more active in sections 05, 09, and 15.

Judgement is treated constructively as experienced read, contextual recognition, and tacit pattern recognition. The report does not call it a defect. It also avoids over-promoting Judgement: the development edge is to bring it forward enough to inform the decision, not to make it the new lead signal.

## 11. Benchmark Scalability Audit

**Score: 8.4/10**

The section structure works. The tone is scalable. The rank-role explanations are useful as a pattern for future reports. Sections 07, 08, 12, and 14 are especially strong models for how future reports can translate ranked signals into reader value.

The biggest scalability risk is language repetition. If future reports imitate the current phrasing too closely, the 24-report set will overuse phrases like `credible basis`, `usable path`, `experienced read`, and `before the path closes`. The second risk is structural repetition: Value, Strengths, and Closing currently overlap enough that future reports may blur these sections unless the benchmark is sharpened first.

What must be fixed before scaling:

- Establish a stronger phrase-variety standard.
- Clarify how Value differs from Strengths.
- Make Closing more distinctive and less recap-driven.
- Add concrete workplace examples without making the report role-specific.
- Make lower-ranked signals actively useful, not only risks or corrective inputs.

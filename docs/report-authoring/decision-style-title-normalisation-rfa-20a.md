# Decision Style Title Normalisation — RFA-20A

## 1. Purpose

This document records the title normalisation decisions for the 24 Decision Style report drafts after all first-draft reports and the full-set consistency audit were complete.

This is title-only. Report files were not renamed. Report body content was not rewritten. The only report-file changes were `working_title` in frontmatter and the top-level `#` heading.

Title normalisation prepares the set for RFA-19 full-set polish and later report-template compilation.

## 2. Title Principles Used

The title set was normalised using these principles:

- Use plain language.
- Keep every title decision-specific.
- Remove duplicate titles.
- Reduce near-duplicate titles.
- Avoid archetype labels.
- Avoid gimmicky or mystical titles.
- Avoid mechanical signal stacking where it reads awkwardly.
- Default to `Decision Maker`.
- Align each title with rank order and the report opening.
- Preserve the benchmark title unless there is a strong reason to change it.
- Keep rank 1 visible enough that the title does not point to the wrong family.
- Let RFA-19 handle opening/body polish after the title system is stable.

## 3. Previous Title Risk Summary

RFA-18 identified these title risks:

- duplicate titles across families
- cross-family collisions
- `Decider` vs `Decision Maker` inconsistency
- overuse of `Responsible` in Standards-led titles
- weak `Evidence-and-Read` title
- mechanical `Experience-and-Responsibility` title where it ended in `Decider`
- framework-like `Experience-to-Action` and `Experience-Shaped Action`
- signal-name-heavy `Practical Standards`
- title/body mismatch risks where the title over-emphasised rank 2 or sounded like another family

RFA-20A removes the duplicate titles, removes all `Decider` endings, preserves the benchmark title, and gives every report a unique `Decision Maker` title.

## 9. Duplicate and Collision Check

No duplicate titles remain.

Near-duplicate risks are reduced but not fully eliminated. The deliberate near-neighbours are:

- `The Fact-Checked Practical Decision Maker` and `The Evidence-Backed Practical Decision Maker`
- `The Experience-Led Action Decision Maker` and `The Experience-Led Practical Decision Maker`
- `The Responsibility-and-Evidence Decision Maker` and `The Evidence-Backed Responsibility Decision Maker`
- `The Practical Responsibility Decision Maker` and `The Responsible Practical Decision Maker`

These near-neighbours map to close rank-order neighbours and should be reviewed in RFA-19 for body alignment and in final title QA for user-facing clarity.

No title now points clearly to the wrong rank-1 family. Some titles still intentionally include rank 2 language because the title must differentiate within a family.

No title overstates rank 2 enough to block RFA-19. The highest watch items are:

- `The Experience-Guided Responsibility Decision Maker`
- `The Practical Responsibility Decision Maker`
- `The Experience-Guided Practical Decision Maker`
- `The Practical Experience-Led Decision Maker`

`Decision Maker` convention is now consistent across all 24 titles. There are no deliberate `Decider` exceptions.

## 10. Body Alignment Notes for RFA-19

RFA-19 should handle these title/body alignment items:

- Echo `The Evidence-and-Action Decision Maker` more naturally in the opening or Key insight without overstating Practicality.
- Ensure `The Evidence-to-Practice Decision Maker` remains Evidence-led and does not sound Practicality-led.
- Make the distinction between `The Fact-Checked Practical Decision Maker` and `The Evidence-Backed Practical Decision Maker` visible in openings.
- Check whether `The Practical Experience-Led Decision Maker` overstates Judgement as rank 2.
- Check whether `The Experience-Guided Practical Decision Maker` sounds too Judgement-led.
- Check whether `The Practical Responsibility Decision Maker` and `The Responsible Practical Decision Maker` are distinct enough in body copy.
- Make Strengths and Closing sections more distinctive now that titles are stable.
- Reduce repeated family anchor words exposed by the new titles: action, practical, responsibility, experience, evidence.
- Preserve the benchmark title and use it as the anchor for opening polish.

Do not perform these body edits in RFA-20A.

# Reader-First Section Purpose Guide

This guide defines the reader job for each visible result section. The authoring schema may use numbered dataset sections, but the report should read as a clear journey from context to application.

## Introduction

Set the domain context. Explain what the domain measures, why it matters, what the reader should pay attention to, and how to use the interpretation without treating it as a fixed label.

## Pattern at a Glance

Give the reader fast recognition of the ranked pattern. Summarise the signal order, score shape, and overall pattern in plain language.

## Core Interpretation

State the central meaning of the pattern. Explain what the combination tends to create, where its value lies, and what tension or trade-off may come with it.

## Signal Profile

Explain each signal's role in the pattern according to rank position. Show how dominant, secondary, tertiary, and least-expressed signals each contribute to the overall behaviour.

## What Shapes This Pattern

Describe the conditions, preferences, and protective logic that make the pattern appear. Help the reader see why the pattern is coherent rather than random.

## How the Pattern Works

Explain the mechanics of the pattern in motion: what starts it, what sustains it, what it protects, and how the signals interact under normal pressure.

## What Comes Easily

Name the strengths the pattern naturally supports. Keep these practical and behavioural, not flattering or generic.

## Where It Can Narrow

Name the predictable narrowing risks. Frame them as overuse, underuse, missing range, or context mismatch rather than fixed weaknesses.

## How to Use It

Give direct application guidance. Cover when to lean into the pattern, what to watch for, and how to develop range without rejecting the core pattern.

## Take Forward

Close with a compact integration: the core gift, core trap, development edge, and a memorable line the reader can carry into action.
